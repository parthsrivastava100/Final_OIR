Shastri Indo-Canadian Institute has announced its programmes for the year 2018-19. It's a reminder call as the deadline is approaching fast and only 15 days are left to submit the proposals. Applicants are encouraged to submit proposals timely to avoid last minute rush. The last minute submission creates pressure on the Grant Management System (GMS) which results in slow processing or inactive sessions.

The various programmes where scholars/faculty/educational administrators can apply are namely:

1. Shastri Institutional Collaborative Research Grant (SICRG)

2. Shastri Mobility Programme (SMP)-for Academicians & Administrators

3. Shastri Research Student Fellowship (SRSF) - for a) Doctoral, b)Postdoctoral and c)Students

4. Shastri Programme Development Grant (SPDG)

5. Shastri Publication Grant (SPG)

6. Shastri Membership Development Grant (SMDG)

7. Faculty-In-Residence Programme (FIR)/ Global Initiative For Academic Network (GIAN)

In general applications from all domains of studies like Social Sciences and Humanities, Arts, Commerce & Management and STEM subjects are accepted. However, applications are encouraged in the focus areas of SICI and priority areas (https://www.shastriinstitute.org/sites/default/files/Priority%20Areas%20Document%20-Brief.pdf) of MHRD, Govt. of India i.e. Food Security and Sustainable Development, Energy, Environmental Science and Climate Change, Agricultural Science & Technology, Health Care Sciences, Nursing, Marine Sciences and Ocean Studies, Information & Communication Technology, Sustainable Habitat, Nano-technology Hardware, Water Resources & River Systems, Advanced Materials, Manufacturing, Security & Defence, Gender, Bi-lateral Trade and Investment, Securing Future of Children and Youth, Higher Education,Teacher Education and Training.

For More details visit our web portal www.shastriinstitute.org (https://www.shastriinstitute.org/) .Applications are to be submitted through the Grant Management System http://gms2.shastriinstitute.org/login. In case of any difficulty in submission, scholars can contact the Shastri India office for assistance. Deadline to submit applications for SICRG, SMP and SRSF is 27th June, 2018 and for SMDG, SPDG and http://www.sici.org.in/uploads/Coming%20soon.pdfSPG is July 25, 2018

FAQ's

1) I am an IIT student, how do I proceed? Ans: Find a faculty member at SFU in your area of interest and contact them with your CV and expression of interest. You will also need an 'anchor' for the internship on the IIT-end so please find a faculty member at IIT willing to supervise you from that side. It would be useful if you can look at the MITACS forms above, fill them out and send them along with your emails to both the faculty contacts (at SFU and IIT). http://www.sfu.ca/engineering.html http://www.sfu.ca/computing.html http://www.sfu.ca/mechatronics.html

2) What level of students are eligible? Ans: The exchange is open to all IIT students, undergraduate or graduate. Undergraduate students in the third year or beyond will get preference but undergraduate students in the second year are also encouraged to apply. All graduate students, Master's or PhD are eligible and encouraged to seek a host lab at SFU for their research.

3) Is there a deadline? Ans: This exchange is open all year around, till the seats allocated are filled up. For undergraduate students looking to visit Vancouver in summer period to coincide with your summer holidays, it would be important to start early. Normally, MITACS will take 4 weeks to approve completed paperwork filed with them. The visa process in India will take some time.Factor these times to get an idea of when you have to start to seek SFU faculty interest to apply.

4) Who do I contact? Ans: See point (1) above. Find your host lab from these links and contact your host lab Professor directly with your CV. http://www.sfu.ca/computing.html http://www.sfu.ca/mechatronics.html Find an anchor at the IIT side, a Professor their who can supervise your internship from that end.

5) The MITACS link shows the deadline has passed. Ans: This program is a parallel new program to the MITACS GRA program and it is specific to IIT and SFU student exchange. It uses the same template forms as the regular MITACS GRA but it is open all year around. Do not worry about deadline being passed.

6) Is this exchange open to any lab at SFU? Ans: Yes, you can find any supervisor at SFU whose research you are interested in working on. Faculty of Applied Science interns will however get an additional stipend of $2000 CAD.

7) Where do I send completed applications? Ans: Applicants should submit completed application packages to SFU’s Mitacs representative Allison Brennan at mitacsbd@sfu.ca with a cc to Mirza Faisal Beg ( mfbeg@sfu.ca) if you are applying to a FAS faculty member.
